History of the United States Virgin Islands (USVI)

The United States Virgin Islands (USVI) are a group of islands in the Caribbean Sea. They are located east of Puerto Rico and are an unincorporated territory of the United States. Here is a brief overview of their history:

Ancient Inhabitants

The Virgin Islands were originally inhabited by the Ciboney, Carib, and Arawak Indians. Christopher Columbus is credited with discovering the islands on his second voyage to the Americas in 1493.

Colonial Era

The islands were settled by the Danish in the 17th century and were known as the Danish West Indies. Sugar plantations were established, and African slaves were brought to work on these plantations.

Transfer to the United States

In 1917, the United States purchased the Virgin Islands from Denmark for $25 million in gold. This transfer is now celebrated annually as Transfer Day on March 31st.

Modern Era

Today, the US Virgin Islands are a popular tourist destination known for their beautiful beaches, rich culture, and history.
